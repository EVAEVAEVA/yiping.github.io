How it all started

With the rising demand in personalisation, human- & care- centricity, patient experience & patient unmet needs are gaining growing tractions and significance during the healthcare solution development. Conventionally, pharmaceutical companies could not have direct connections with patients due to compliances and regulations. The existing digital solutions are siloed and don't follow the same standards, such that there's no interoperability. To revolutionise the customer & patient engagement, our partners in the Medical Affairs team in Japan reached out to the Johnson & Johnson Design (JJD) team to leverage on our expertise in research, patient (Human) experiences, strategy & service design and solution testing & evaluations.
The vision, is to synergise the power of data & digitisation, the scalability across TAs, as well as improvements in patient experiences.

Phase 1 - Medication Adherence & Proof of Concept validation

Context

Before JJD is onboarded, our business partners have worked with Deloitte team for a preliminary proposal - including insights from discovery sessions with the Health-Care Providers (HCP) and Patients (PT) across 6 Disease Areas (DA) in 4 Therapeutic Areas (TA).

Based on the proposal, the focus area for phase 1 is around PT's medication adherence and the target DA is schizophrenia patients. When we were brought in, the main scope for JJD team is

  • To test a Proof of Concept on an automatic medication tracking solution

    • The PoC, including the planning & preparation and final report read-out, was over the timespan of Sep - Nov 2021.

    Analysis

    Discovery phase includes a large portion of secondary research & desktop research. As everyone on the team are new to the pharma industry, we started by diving deeper into the context and problem space. Including

  • Internal & external stakeholder mapping and understanding
  • Top level pharma & key terms familiarisations
  • Patient journey understanding for Schizoprenia patients
  • Problem space deepdive (medication adherence, as well as other problem areas in the PT experience journey)
  • Other solutions than automatic medication trackers
  • Competitor & market analysis
    • Planning & conduct

    Together with the business partners, we recruited 20 internal users to validate the usability & effectiveness of Popit - an automatic medication tracker in accompany of a mobile app. The study spanned a duraction of 21 days to observe the participants' patterns overtime.

  • Planning with control groups & scheduling
  • Test material preparation
  • Facilitation
  • Result consolidation & synthesis
  • Note that the quantitative result analysis were done by the vendor, who have direct access to the collected mobile data from this test.
    • Synthesis

    What we discovered is that patients aren't adhereing to treatments for various reasons, including unbearable side-effects, unable to fit into their schedules, inconvenience at public places, and most common of all, general forgetfulness. Taking into consideration of the behavior design, we factored in some of the related questions into the test planning & surveys. Other test objectives include:

  • Effectiveness of reminders in medication tracking
  • Accuracy & effectiveness of a hardware device in medication tracking
  • Any differences in using software for medication tracking
  • Any differences in having regular HCP check-in during medication tracking
  • Other factors that affected the participants' behaviors
  • General feedbacks on the PoC
  • Other concerns, inputs & suggestions on medication adherence
    • Result

    Overall, while this solution is novice as it combines hardware with a software solution, and the aim is for tracking to be done automatically, such that patient don't need to put in additional effort of logging their medications. However, the experience of use is not smooth, involves manual effort of set-up, accuracy issues etc.; and considering the condition of patients in our target user group (Schizophrenia patients - forgetfulness is a big issue), the solution doesn't prove to solve for that.

    However, we uncovered other insights, such as how factors like habits & motivations affected the participants behavior, perceptions of HCP & data privacy changes the receptiveness towards certain actions, suggestions on other solutions that could help the participants with additional accountability, etc.

    Phase 2 - Strategic proposal & solution selection playbook

    Context

    We divided into two workstreams - with the strategic consultant focusing on the DM & digitilisation aspects research, and me along with the juniors focusing on the epxeriences & solution space. The subsequent contents in phase 2 will focus on my workstream.

    Discover

    Additional topics in the Discover section includes

  • Patient journey understanding across TA/DA
  • Problem space deepdive (medication adherence, as well as other problem areas in the PT experience journey)
  • Other solutions, competitor & market analysis
  • Patient criterias & the impact on their behavior, mindset & solution outcomes
    • Define

    Define stage happened in parallel to the discover phase as well as the PoC validation preparation. The experience & solution workstream focused on analysing the PT experience across different TA/DAs, synthesising on the common problem areas, ideating & research for potential solutions, as well as analysing factors that affect the effectiveness of solutions.

    7 common areas of problems emerged from the PT experience journey, including Detection, Information gap , Diagnosis & treatment decision making , Behavior related gaps , Emotional needs, Costs ; all of which could be mapped to the 5 types of solution areas - Awareness/info, HCP-PT communications, PT support, Sensors & tracking , Emerging healthcare solutions & services . We started the synthesis by consolidating the insights to a TA/DA agnostic patient experience journey map to provide a holistic outlook --

    A key insight emerged from the research is the significance of Patient Segment & Personalisation. These factors affects the effectiveness of implementing any solutions. We have identified the correlations between different patient characteristics and the impact on the solutions.

    The next step of the process includes evaluating the solutions' value and outcome, apart from effectiveness for different patient segments. We generated detailed evaluation criteria with a value-based rubric table.

    Design

    The research areas were broad - the team came back together and discuss on how each workstream could be synthesized into insightful, actionable guidelines. Including

  • Development of the 4 stage framework to achieve the partners' vision
  • Development of the 3 step playbook to help stakeholders identify the next best solution option
    • Deliver

    The final stage of delivery really anchored on crafting the most meaningful service playbook by consolidating all strategic proposals & findings. The output, is the PoC validation report as well as an extensive recommendation for next steps. We have broken down the report into a few key sections, including the

  • The overview with a 4 stage strategy framework
  • Data strategy to ensure successful interoperability of digital solutions
  • Patient journey & insight on key problem categories, solution groups, and importance of patient segmentation
  • 3 step playbook to help stakeholders identify the next best solution option
  • Team models to support project at different scope

    • The story is framed around What we're hoping to achieve > what are the building blocks > how we can achieve it along with actionable guidelines.
    Due to the financial & project structure of the company, the continual development of this project is extended to 2022.

    Phase 3 - Pivot! Focus on HCP - PT communications

    Context

    The goal of the stakeholders are still to find an innovative digital solution which solves a patient unmet need. Based on the insight report from phase 2, as well as organisation's business priorities & direct access on customer engagements, we re-evaluated the problem space, brought in customers (HCPs) into the ecosystem, and pivoted the focus from medication adherence to HCP-PT communications.

    Discover

    Combining the results from patient journey, I included the engagement journey with HCPs into an ecosystem map. This is to facilitate identification of opportunity areas with high impact on both customers (HCPs) and PTs.

    High impact stages -

    Define

    A variety of activities happen in-between HCP-PT communication cycle. We hosted a few workshops with the business partners to consolidate all the input & ideas, synthesize alongside with them. We specified the target user group & persona profiles, the stage of journey to target, as well as potential solutions for consideration.

    The focus gradually conversed to emotional AI analysis for patient, as well as trainings & education (primarily for HCPs).

    Design & further development

    Design & proposals evolved over time. AR/VR solutions, comprehensive emotional AI & new forms of HCP trainings.

    After discussion with the Japan partners, we decided to focus less on the new digital mediums like AR/VR, but focus more on using AI and other tools for PT emotional status analysis. Hence the team did more research and iterated the proposals further.

    Deliver

    As part of the deliverables, we crafted 4 sets of comprehensive proposal, with the broader focus on IBD & PC patients and solve for their painpoints during communications -

  • Using LINE ecosystem & existing medical information content for better PT & HCP education
  • Leveraging on existing digital technologies to analyse PT emotion while collecing datapoints
  • AI/ML & sentiment analysis on existing JP patient forums
  • Crafting gamified training courses for HCPs to improve their empathy during PT interactions

    • Towards Q3 2022, our business partners were involved in a separate workstream on patient engagement programs, shifting their focus area to more specific, Prostate Cancer patients & Adverse Effect reporting. This allowed us to extend proposal 1 & 2 in more details, together with examples on companies in Japan to be considered as partners.

    The takeaway

    Project planning - Establish clear goal, even with ambiguity

    This is my first project after joining Johnson & Johnson - dived straight in right after the first day. The project had a lot of ambiguity & fluidity. For example, the role of my team was never clearly defined, which allowed us to broaden scope & stretch the influence of Design with flexibility and in various roles & responsibilities.

    This is also my first experience to lead while working with the business stakeholders in partnerships. It took a while of defining & adapting my role at the beginning of the project to get used to this working model. Beyond leading a small team, phase 3 of the project has seen my growth in autonomously leading & guiding the partner discussions, hosting workshops, as well as shaping the project directions.

    The project expanded my workscope to design strategy during business problem & solution definition - which has also been my target growth area for 2021. While the ambiguity of the project can be unnerving at times, it has been a great and rapid learning opportunity to identify my own patterns, methods, approaches & mental models in strategic projects.

    Processes - Onboarding

    Proper project onboarding is critical. The process itself requires user-centricity design. For example, who are we intending to onboard, and how much prior knowledge do they have?
    My own onboarding process to the project was a steep learning curve, with no prior pharmatheutical nor J&J knowledge, and only one onboarding session.
    Project that has a strategic focus is harder to explain and handover, compared to those with very visible outputs. Sometimes it takes effort to explain the development of project and the decision making process inbetween.
    Proper documentation, clear explanations, and no presumption on the audience is very critical.

    Processes - Diverge, but not too much

    Partially due to the ambuiguity, undefined project deliverables, and general amitiousness, the discovery process took a long while and many twists and turns. The diverge and converge process happened various times - looking at medical adherences, relevant solutions, broader problem space, common opportunity areas, etc. There was a tendency to wish to tackle too many problems in one go - which resulted in a high time expenditure on insights that weren't used in the later part of the project.

    Communications - working with remote teams & cultural nuances

    We were very grateful of the project partners in Japan - the partners are very willing to listen and hear us out. Communication was not easy. Most sessions require interpretors and pre-work. During my 1-1 with direct counter-parts, it was easy to observe that only 60% of information get translated and delivered. There were a lot of clarifications during the sessions. While presenting ideas in English, we also tend to forget that translations in Japanese has longer sentence structure.
    It takes a while to get used to the effective pace and contents of communications. Cultural nuances would also need to be taken into considerations, such as preparing apt activities and sharing sessions in workshops, so we could receive adequate share-outs from Japanese colleagues.

    Activities - Hosting workshops

    We hosted a lot of remote workshops in this project. Normally, my first instinct in planning workshops is to FILL IN a session with planned activities and contents. We tend to overlook the importance of pauses and breaks, pace and emotional energy. Breaks in workshops are as cirtical as negative spaces in architecture, without which the session would not be a comprehensive experience. Emotional energy are like circulations that guides the flow of communications & discussion. It need to be smooth, diverse, feels natural and fluent for collaborative sessions, or attention grabbing enough for structured share-outs.
