Design Process

1. Background Research

What are the social interactive robots currently in the market?

2. Project Scope

Confirm on the type of robot I want to focus on and to which stage do I want to accomplish.

3. System Architecture

Construct the systematic architecture, including the hardware and software.

4. Sketch & Idealisation

This is the best process during which you could fully unleash your imagination!

5. Model & Render

To construct the idea in 3D and see it come alive with some touch-up.

6. Prototype & Assembly

Construct ta raw prototype using 3D printing and some exisiting components.

7. Software

A collaboration and developmnet of the software requirement for Babble.

8. Design Improvement

When things go wrong, sometimes we need to readjust and make it improve.

Step 1. Background Research

By the time I did the research, there were the few social interactive robots in the market that are rather famous. This diagram is constructed as an intuitive representation of how the robots have a focus on different user groups, and the yellow-shadowed segment represents the target group of Babble, which is as a friend/companion for Asian market.

Step 2.
Project Scope

The four major segments listed on the diagram resembles four of the most important segments while performing social interactive robot design, incluing Architecture, Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), Cognition & Perception, as well as Action. Above all factors, there is design and morphology that directly linked with the most intuitive impression on Babble.

Step 3.
System Architecture

Systematic components, the hardware and software could be grouped according to major functional groups or the input/output requirement for Babble. This diagram has divided according to the Input/output of Babble, and from which the deriviation of its software and hardware requirements.

Step 4.
Sketch & Idealisation

One sketch diagram of Babble, in which the major dimension and shape is settled for Babble.

Step 5.
Model & Render

Babble is modelled to 3D in SolidWorks, and thereafter rendered in Keyshot to produce the realistic outlook.

Step 6.
Assembly & Prototype

Based on the hardware connectors and components that were previously available, Babble is assembled to test for the validity of its hardware design.
