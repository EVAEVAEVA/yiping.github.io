How it all started

Our business partners in the MedTech business pillar wishes to push for adoption and use of new digital mediums, such as the realm of extended realities.
This project is funded by our partners in DePuy Synthes Hip and Supply Chain, in preparation for a brand showcase and workshop. The goal is to broaden the mindset of sales representatives and business teams towards new tools and Mixed Reality medium adoption.
I was selected by the project lead (Lead of Strategy) to lead the design of the project, after sharing my learnings from the AR/VR course from MIT | xPro with the team.

"Storytelling via a new digital medium"

· Mixed reality ·

The ask

Our project lead set the direction - to showcasing an oesteoporosis hip case with virual surgical simulation via a hololens. While the business partners prefer mixed reality over virtual reality, the goal is to have the story as immersive as possible.

The initial approach

While working on this project, I was taking the "Storytelling for influence" course with IDEOU, and decided to put this project into practice. The learnings from the course shaped my initial approach -

  • Identify the key message (the big idea)
  • Identify key moments in nugget formats
  • Identify the story arc
  • Reconstruct and sequence the key moments to build the blueprint
  • Test and validate the big idea

    • The first review

    In the initial version, we followed the 4 C's story arc and proposed a story of a grandma going through a series of day-to-day actiivties. The climax is when she takes a bus that stops abruptly and experience a fracture. And the resolution is via the virtual surgical simulation procedure.

    The feedback, was that the story focuses too much on the grandma and oesteoporosis, which makes the key message derails from the original intent (i.e.: mixed reality medium can be used in MedTech brand in various different ways)

    Iterations

    Instead of going back to follow the same approach as initial phase, I decided to take a step back and do more discovery and ideation - starting by listing down some key emotional messages and gather more inspirations.

    With the key emotional messages and new inspirations, we further crafted 5 different story approaches.

    Story idea Key message
    A story within a story: Grandma talking to the kid about her surgery (metaphorical, with animations) Ease of understanding, simple
    A few day to day moments scenes with simple revelation at the end: Audience finds out she’s a post-surgery grandma who no longer worries abt the Osteo (mini tensions) Empowerment (grandma)
    From the hip/ bone’s pov (feat. the hip); journey of a hip; overlay to see bone deteoriates etc. Empowerment (joint)
    A world without mobility (single person, center stage experience) or a world where we can’t bend (view a virtual story) Empower/Inspire
    Contrasting scenes to break stereotypes of being old/ of the surgery or mobility/ of tech Inspire & possibilities

    Further development

    With consideration of feasibility & timeline, as well as the emotional impact of each proposals, we narrowed down the option to the third story, "From the hip/ bone’s pov (feat. the hip); journey of a hip; overlay to see bone deteoriates etc."

    Final refinement

    The audio sample for storyline 3 was impactful. Having the recording helps with the emotional message delivery, and makes the context to feel more immersive. However, featuring the bone as the main character is too abstract - hence we made some further adjustment to the storyline, and started more detailed storyboarding.

    The high-fidelity storyboard is handed over to our vendor who continued to develop and render in Unity.

    The takeaway

    Structured framework & dynamic stories

    Good to have a framework - but frameworks are to guide ways of thinking than ways of works.
