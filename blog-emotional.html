The modern day emotional pandemic

We might be moving to an emotional “crisis”. Heightened emotions and highly exaggerated and arbitrary matters are rushing to us while we stay connected to the world.
The intense and frequent triggers make our brain fatigue, just like any other drugs, and could affect our threshold towards pain and tragedies.
This crisis is not acute pain, but a chronic illness that would slowly creep up on us, of which the consequences we do not yet know.
For example, smart personal devices have created social-distancing and isolation without the help of the Covid pandemic.
Instagram began to hide the “likes” features in certain countries for users’ mental well-being.
Speed and efficiency have become so widespread that loading a web-page for more than 5 seconds would annoy us.
In the context of Singapore, a power outage would likely to create a panic towards no aircon than no lighting.
Sooner or later we may even forget how to drive, or even climb stairs for more than 5 steps.
The effect of emotions is way farreaching than that. Looking back on racism, holocaust and extreme events happened in human history.
The effect of emotions were substantial.
